## ⚠️ PRE-STAGE-3 TASK (decided — ready to execute): pull the fills out of the scale

**Observed by owner 2026-06-23, direction locked same day.** The surface scale
promised a monotonic ramp 1→10, but positions **9/10 are the emphasis fills, not
scale steps** — the yellow band (and signals' bright fill) exposed it. The fix is
a rename: scale = 1–8, fills become `cta-1/2` + `highlight-1/2` roles. Full spec
below; execute before Stage 3.

### Why it happens
`highlight-9/10` are generated as ladder rungs and then **darkened to the
WCAG-4.5 white-text edge** — EXCEPT within 1σ of `YELLOW_L_LIFT.centerH` (H 92 ±
20°), where darkening would destroy the hue (the warning-signal precedent). So:
- **Non-yellow** highlight-9 is darkened to ~L 0.53–0.59, carries **white** text.
- **Yellow** highlight-9 is left bright at ~L 0.62–0.65, carries **black** text.

Concrete (light mode, from `npm run highlight-audit`):

| brand | accent-8 L | highlight-9 L | highlight-10 L | on-highlight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Blueberry (blue) | 0.738 | 0.564 | 0.519 | white |
| Matcha (green) | 0.738 | 0.540 | 0.493 | white |
| Golden Milk (yellow) | 0.763 | **0.645** | **0.604** | **black** |
| Chamomile (yellow) | — | **0.650** | **0.610** | **black** |

Net effect: positions 9/10 were presented as uniform surface-scale slots, but
they're really the emphasis **fills** — and a fill can't be a darkening scale
step (yellow stays bright; a signal's fill IS its bright identity). That exposed
the mislabel. Resolved below.

### Resolved direction (locked 2026-06-23) — fills come out of the scale

**The scale is 1–8** (`paper`/`wash`/`accent`), kept **globally numbered** (not
per-category) on purpose: it preserves headroom for interaction/overlap colors
between categories later. The two emphasis fills are pulled out as **roles**,
numbered `-1` (base) / `-2` (hover) and restarting from 1 (`n+1` = hover) — the
same move already made for `ink` and `cta`.

**No `fill-` prefix.** The register words (paper/wash/accent) vs role words
(cta/highlight) already separate scale from fills; bare names stay consistent
with the other unprefixed roles (`ink`/`identity`) and preserve the
universal-vs-role parallel already in `on-highlight` (universal) / `on-cta` (role).

**Rename map** (only the two fill pairs change):

| now | locked |
|---|---|
| `paper-1/2` `wash-3/4/5` `accent-6/7/8` | unchanged (the 1–8 scale) |
| `ink` `ink-alt` | unchanged |
| `cta` / `cta-hover` | `cta-1` / `cta-2` |
| `highlight-9` / `highlight-10` | `highlight-1` / `highlight-2` |
| `on-cta` / `on-highlight` | unchanged |
| `identity` | unchanged |

**Per-ramp inventory:**
- **brand / secondary:** scale 1–8 · `ink-alt` `ink` · `cta-1` `cta-2` `on-cta` · `highlight-1` `highlight-2` `on-highlight` · `identity`
- **neutral:** same, minus `identity` (gray `highlight`, black `cta`)
- **signals:** scale 1–8 · `ink-alt` `ink` · `highlight-1` `highlight-2` `on-highlight` — **no cta** (the alert IS `highlight-1`; the structural "no error button")

**Why both fills stay on brands (we did NOT drop the brand highlight):** a brand
needs a fill that reliably holds **white** text at any hue. A bright `cta` can't
(a yellow cta needs black text); the darkened `highlight` is that white-text
fill. Verified with a yellow-vs-pink export (2026-06-23). So `cta` = the
identity/action fill, `highlight` = the guaranteed-white-text emphasis fill —
both are needed.

**Why this fixes the yellow/signal break:** there's no "position 9/10" left to
imply the bright fill is a darkening scale step. `highlight-1` is a role, so
bright-for-yellow / dark-for-pink is expected (just like `cta` already varies by
hue). The 1–8 scale stays honestly monotonic.

### It's a rename — low risk
No value or engine-math change: the brand `highlight` is still generated exactly
as today (the white-text-edge logic is *why* it exists), signals keep their fill,
neutral keeps its black button. Touch points: `tokenNames` (stop→name map +
`TOKEN_ORDER`), the two emitters (mechanical), `tokens/semantic.css` re-point,
plugin detection/alias literals, `figma-verify`, `highlight-audit`. The blessed
`highlight-snapshot.json` stays valid (values unchanged). Gate: audit
byte-identical, highlight-audit, figma-verify, all builds green; no leaked old
names; plugin idempotency holds.

Do this BEFORE Stage 3 (the demo cards visualize the scale). Independent of
Stage 2.5 (green signal), which is a value change, not a rename.
