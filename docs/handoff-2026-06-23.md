## ⚠️ TASK (do BEFORE Stage 3): yellow brands don't get a real scale at 9/10

**Observed by owner 2026-06-23.** The surface scale promises a monotonic
lightness ramp 1→10, but at positions **9/10 the yellow band breaks it**, which
may force a naming/model rethink.

### Why it happens
`highlight-9/10` are generated as ladder rungs and then **darkened to the
WCAG-4.5 white-text edge** — EXCEPT within 1σ of `YELLOW_L_LIFT.centerH` (H 92 ±
20°), where darkening would destroy the hue (the warning-signal precedent). So:
- **Non-yellow** highlight-9 is darkened to ~L 0.53–0.59, carries **white** text.
- **Yellow** highlight-9 is left bright at ~L 0.62–0.65, carries **black** text.

Concrete (light mode, from `npm run highlight-audit`):

| brand | accent-8 L | highlight-9 L | highlight-10 L | on-highlight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Blueberry (blue) | 0.738 | 0.564 | 0.519 | white |
| Matcha (green) | 0.738 | 0.540 | 0.493 | white |
| Golden Milk (yellow) | 0.763 | **0.645** | **0.604** | **black** |
| Chamomile (yellow) | — | **0.650** | **0.610** | **black** |

### The actual problems
1. **Weak / non-uniform scale step.** Yellow's accent-8→highlight-9 drop is small
   (0.763→0.645), so 9/10 barely darken — the "scale" plateaus instead of
   continuing down. Each ramp is individually monotonic (the audit checks this),
   but the *register* of position 9/10 is inconsistent across hues.
2. **White-label remap breaks.** "highlight-9" is sold as a uniform scale
   position to remap into any system, but it lands ~0.1 L lighter for yellows and
   flips text polarity (white→black). A consumer remapping `highlight-9` gets a
   materially different thing depending on hue.
3. **Naming tension.** Numbering them `highlight-9/10` implies clean scale
   positions; for yellow they behave more like a fixed bright role fill. Hence
   the owner's note that this "may cause us to need to address the naming."

### Options to weigh (decide before Stage 3 — the demo cards will visualize the
scale, so the model must be settled first)
- **A. Accept + document** the yellow exception as inherent (hue-preservation
  beats scale-uniformity); keep names, note the caveat.
- **B. Darken yellow anyway** to a true scale register and accept black text
  there (uniform lightness, non-uniform text polarity).
- **C. Rework the model/naming** so 9/10 aren't presented as uniform scale
  positions when they can't be (e.g. treat highlight as a role, not a numbered
  scale slot; or split "scale slot" vs "emphasis fill").
- This is the SAME white-text-enforcement mechanism as **Stage 2.5** (green
  signal). Resolve them together — whatever rule lands here should also drive 2.5.
